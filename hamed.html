*Types of Intel processors in order

1. The economy category for simple tasks

  • *These processors are designed for internet browsing, studying, and using Office applications.
  • .Intel Celeron: The least expensive and least powerful, suitable for very basic tasks.
  • .Intel Pentium: Slightly better than Celeron, good for educational laptops.
  • .Intel Processor: The new name Intel recently introduced to replace Pentium and Celeron in modern devices.

2. General and Advanced Use Category Intel Core i

  • *This is the most popular and widespread category, ranked as follows:
  • Core i5: The mid-range and "value king", excellent for gaming, programming, and intensive daily work.
  • Core i3: For home or office users: browsing, movies, light software.
  • Core i7: For high performance, ideal for designers, heavy gamers, and content creators.
  • Core i9: The pinnacle of performance in this series, designed for very demanding tasks such as 3D rendering and high-definition live streaming of games.

3. The new Intel Core Ultra series

  • *Intel recently began using the Ultra designation for processors that contain Neural Processing Units (NPUs) to support artificial intelligence and improve energy efficiency:
  • Core Ultra 5
  • Core Ultra 7
  • Core Ultra 9

4. Professional category and Intel Xeon servers

  • Intel Xeon: It is not aimed at the average home user, but rather at workstations and servers, and is characterized by its ability to work for very long periods and support for huge RAM.

*Important note about 'generations' and 'letters'When purchasing, the order alone is not enough; you need to pay attention to two things:

  • .Generation: A 14th-generation i3 processor may be stronger than a 10th-generation i7 processor. Always look for newer generations such as 12th, 13th, or 14th.

*The letters at the end of the model:

  • U: Energy saver for long battery life in the laptop
  • H / HX: High performance for gaming and editing on laptops
  • K: Capable of overclocking for high-performance desktop devices
  • F: Does not have an internal graphics card requires an external graphics card
